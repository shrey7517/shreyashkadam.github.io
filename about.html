About Me

I am an Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering student with a strong interest in designing intelligent systems that combine hardware, simulation, and software. I enjoy understanding how systems work at a fundamental level and then translating that understanding into practical, working solutions.

My academic journey has given me hands-on exposure to embedded systems, Arduino-based prototyping, MATLAB and Simulink simulations, and early-stage machine learning applications. I believe simulation is a powerful tool to predict and improve real-world behavior before physical implementation.

Beyond technical skills, I value discipline, clarity, and calm decision- making under pressure. My involvement in innovation cells, national-level competitions, and leadership roles has helped me develop strong communication and teamwork abilities.

I aim to grow as a systems-oriented engineer who can bridge theory and implementation, contributing to impactful engineering solutions in areas such as embedded intelligence, control systems, and communication technologies.
